# ACTIVITY 1: GUESS THE TOKENS

<br>

### How many tokens does each sentence use?
### Write your guess on your whiteboard!

<br>

**Rules:** Closest guess wins. Remember: 1 token is roughly 3/4 of a word.

**Time:** 10 minutes

<br>

---

<br>

## ROUND 1:

<br>

# "Hello, world!"

<br>

### How many tokens? Write your guess...

<br>

---

<br>

## ROUND 1 ANSWER:

<br>

```
    "Hello, world!"

    [ Hello ] [ , ] [ _world ] [ ! ]

    ANSWER: 4 tokens
```

<br>

---

<br>

## ROUND 2:

<br>

# "The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog."

<br>

### How many tokens?

<br>

---

<br>

## ROUND 2 ANSWER:

<br>

```
    "The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog."

    [The] [_quick] [_brown] [_fox] [_jumps] [_over] [_the] [_lazy] [_dog] [.]

    ANSWER: 10 tokens
```

<br>

---

<br>

## ROUND 3:

<br>

# "Supercalifragilisticexpialidocious"

<br>

### One word. But how many tokens?

<br>

---

<br>

## ROUND 3 ANSWER:

<br>

```
    "Supercalifragilisticexpialidocious"

    [Super] [cal] [if] [rag] [il] [istic] [exp] [ial] [id] [ocious]

    ANSWER: ~10 tokens (one word, many pieces!)
```

<br>

### Long, unusual words get chopped into more tokens.

# ACTIVITY 2: TWO TRUTHS AND A LIE -- AI EDITION

<br>

### Three statements about AI. One is false.
### Hold up 1, 2, or 3 fingers for the lie!

**Time:** 10 minutes

<br>

---

<br>

## ROUND 1:

<br>

### 1. AI can generate images from text descriptions.

### 2. AI understands what it's saying the same way humans do.

### 3. AI models can have over a trillion parameters.

<br>

---

<br>

## ROUND 1 ANSWER:

<br>

# NUMBER 2 IS THE LIE.

<br>

### AI doesn't "understand" -- it predicts patterns.
### It's very convincing, but there's no comprehension inside.

<br>

---

<br>

## ROUND 2:

<br>

### 1. ChatGPT was trained on more text than any human could read in a lifetime.

### 2. AI can perfectly fact-check its own answers.

### 3. The word "hallucination" in AI means it makes things up that sound real.

<br>

---

<br>

## ROUND 2 ANSWER:

<br>

# NUMBER 2 IS THE LIE.

<br>

### AI cannot reliably fact-check itself.
### It's confident even when wrong. That's why YOU verify.

<br>

---

<br>

## ROUND 3:

<br>

### 1. AI models need to be retrained to learn new information.

### 2. An AI agent can write code, run it, find bugs, and fix them.

### 3. AI uses more electricity than the entire country of France.

<br>

---

<br>

## ROUND 3 ANSWER:

<br>

# NUMBER 3 IS THE LIE.

<br>

### AI uses a LOT of energy, but not THAT much (yet).
### The other two are true -- agents really can self-debug!

<br>

---

<br>

## ROUND 4:

<br>

### 1. The "temperature" setting controls how creative AI responses are.

### 2. AI can read and write files on your computer without any restrictions.

### 3. Most AI models process text as "tokens," not whole words.

<br>

---

<br>

## ROUND 4 ANSWER:

<br>

# NUMBER 2 IS THE LIE.

<br>

### Sandbox modes restrict what AI can access.
### Without permission, AI can't touch your files.

<br>

---

<br>

## ROUND 5:

<br>

### 1. An AI agent can run in a loop, retrying until it succeeds.

### 2. AI was invented in 2020 by OpenAI.

### 3. "Prompt engineering" is a real job title at tech companies.

<br>

---

<br>

## ROUND 5 ANSWER:

<br>

# NUMBER 2 IS THE LIE.

<br>

### AI research started in the 1950s!
### OpenAI made it mainstream, but the field is decades old.

# ACTIVITY 4: SPOT THE DANGER

<br>

### I'll show a scenario. You identify the safety risk.
### Shout it out!

**Time:** 10 minutes

<br>

---

<br>

## SCENARIO 1:

<br>

```
    A student pastes this into an AI chat:

    "Here's my code. My API key is
     sk-abc123xyz789 and my database
     password is 'fluffy_cat_2024'.
     Please fix the login bug."
```

<br>

### What's the danger?

<br>

---

<br>

## ANSWER:

<br>

# EXPOSED CREDENTIALS!

<br>

### Never paste API keys or passwords into AI.
### Remove them first, use placeholders like "YOUR_KEY_HERE."

<br>

---

<br>

## SCENARIO 2:

<br>

```
    A student gives an AI agent full file system
    access and says: "Clean up my Downloads folder.
    Delete anything that looks old."
```

<br>

### What's the danger?

<br>

---

<br>

## ANSWER:

<br>

# DESTRUCTIVE ACTION WITH NO REVIEW!

<br>

### "Looks old" is vague. The agent might delete important files.
### Always use sandbox mode and review before deleting.

<br>

---

<br>

## SCENARIO 3:

<br>

```
    A student uses AI to write an entire essay,
    copies it word for word, and submits it
    as their own work.
```

<br>

### What's the danger?

<br>

---

<br>

## ANSWER:

<br>

# ACADEMIC DISHONESTY!

<br>

### This is the CHEAT path. You learn nothing.
### Use AI to help you LEARN, not to replace your thinking.

<br>

---

<br>

## SCENARIO 4:

<br>

```
    An AI confidently states:

    "The Great Wall of China is visible
     from the Moon with the naked eye."
```

<br>

### What's the danger?

<br>

---

<br>

## ANSWER:

<br>

# AI HALLUCINATION!

<br>

### This is a common myth, and it's FALSE.
### The Great Wall is NOT visible from the Moon.
### AI states false things with total confidence. Always verify.

# ACTIVITY 8: THE AGENTS.md CHALLENGE

<br>

### Write an AGENTS.md file for an AI agent that
### manages YOUR room or locker!

**Time:** 15 minutes

**Rules:** Include at least 5 instructions. Be specific!

<br>

---

<br>

## TEMPLATE:

<br>

```
    # AGENTS.md -- My Room Manager

    ## Your Role
    You are an AI agent that organizes my [room/locker].

    ## Rules
    1. NEVER throw away _______________
    2. ALWAYS keep _______________ on the _______________
    3. Books go _______________
    4. Dirty clothes go _______________
    5. If you're unsure about something, _______________

    ## Priorities
    - Most important: _______________
    - Least important: _______________

    ## Off Limits
    - Do NOT touch my _______________
    - Do NOT open my _______________
```

<br>

### Be creative! Be specific! The funniest and most
### thorough AGENTS.md wins.

<br>

---

<br>

<br>

    ┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
